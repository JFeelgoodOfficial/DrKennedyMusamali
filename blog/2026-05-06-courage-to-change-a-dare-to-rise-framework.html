Courage to Change: A DARE to RISE Framework

May 6, 2026

November 4, 2025

“When you change the way you look at things, the things you look at change.” — Wayne Dyer

We've all faced disruptive life events—the failed project, the sudden market shift, the personal loss, the unexpected career change. In these moments, our instinct is often to "fix" what's broken and restore what once was. More often than not, this urgency is driven by the fear of losing what feels familiar rather than the courage to face an uncertain future.

But what if restoration is not the goal? What if the old normal is the very thing that needs to change? What if disruption is not an obstacle, but an invitation to transformation? What if the disruptive event is daring you to rise, not despite it, but because of it?

In this article, we share a framework to help you Dare to Rise and turn disruption into transformation. It offers a clear structure for managing disruption through intentional deconstruction. It guides purposeful reconstruction that moves you from uncertainty to clarity and growth.

DARE to Deconstruct

Dare is the challenge to act with courage. DARE to Deconstruct is the courage to do the difficult and uncomfortable work of disassembling accustomed beliefs, roles, and routines that no longer serve us. The process of deconstruction clears the path for transformation.

As a framework, DARE provides structure for dismantling the obstacles that make change difficult. These include ingrained assumptions, entrenched beliefs, and outdated practices. DARE stands for Define, Assess, Regulate, and Explore.

D – Define the Problem: Name the disruptive or triggering event. The act of naming it allows you to reclaim a sense of control.

A – Assess its Impact: Conduct a disruption appraisal. It reveals the roles, routines, and beliefs that no longer serve you and need to be dismantled.

R – Regulate Discomfort: Manage the emotional impact to create space for reflection. This promotes creative rather than reactive decision-making.

E – Explore Emerging Options: Leverage new insights to create a blueprint for reconstruction. A blueprint offers a concrete path forward, making transformation tangible and achievable.

RISE and Reconstruct

While DARE is about deconstruction, RISE is about reconstruction. To rise is to emerge. RISE and Reconstruct refers to the process of rebuilding with intention, clarity, and purpose. Reconstruction completes the transformation.

RISE is a framework that provides structure for rebuilding. It includes reframing assumptions, restructuring beliefs, and habituating practices that support intentional change. RISE stands for Recognize, Imagine, Support, and Embody.

R – Recognize your foundation: Identify internal and external assets. These are the resources that enable you to rebuild from a position of strength, not scarcity.

I – Imagine the desired outcome: Visualize and design a blueprint to achieve results. This creates a clear and compelling target.

S – Support the process: Lean on your support network. This provides encouragement, accountability, and perspective to help navigate challenges and maintain momentum.

E – Embody the change: Embrace and habituate new practices. This transforms change into your identity, moving it from a concept you know to a truth you live.

Change begins with how we see things. Transformation begins with how we think about things. So, when we change the way we think, the things we think about change. In the process, we transform how we do things. DARE to RISE is not just a framework—it’s a call for change and transformation. Take courage and dare to rise.
