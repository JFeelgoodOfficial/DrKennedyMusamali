Beyond Change: Why Transitions Matter

May 6, 2026

August 12, 2025

Welcome to the first edition of The Power of Transition!

I’m thrilled to introduce this new resource. Each issue will explore how we adapt to life’s changes and offer tools to support your mental wellness journey. In this inaugural article, we’ll explore why transitions matter. Let’s begin by examining three key concepts: Change, Transition, and Transformation.

“It is when we are in transition that we are most completely alive.” — William Bridges, Transitions: Making Sense of Life’s Changes (2004, p. 164)

Change vs. Transition

Although the terms change and transition are often used interchangeably, they represent two distinct concepts. Change refers to an altered or modified state triggered by an event. Transition is the process of adapting to that change.

Derived from the Latin term transire, meaning “to go across,” transition involves emotional, psychological, and behavioral adjustments to accommodate a new reality. It entails moving from an old to a new state of being.

For instance, when someone decides to relocate to a new city (the change), they adjust to new friends, navigate different public transport systems, and acclimate to a new environment (the transition). Change rearranges your circumstances externally. Transition rearranges you internally.

What About Transformation?

Now, you might wonder about transformation and how it differs from change. That’s a great question! These terms are also often used interchangeably although they are distinct concepts. While transformations arise from change, they represent a profound and clear departure from the status quo.

A transformation is deeply rooted and involves significant shifts in beliefs, values, and mindsets. For example, the pandemic didn’t just change our routines; it transformed how we work, connect, and define well-being. Similarly, artificial intelligence (AI) is not only changing our tools and processes, but also transforming how we live, learn, and make decisions in real time.

In Summary

Both change and transformation redefine how we engage with ourselves and the systems around us. Each is mediated by periods of transition, which help us navigate adjustment and adaptation.

However, unlike change, transformation alters the very foundations of our beliefs, profoundly shaping how we think, feel, and act. Transformations are holistic, enduring, and identity-shaping. They mark a shift not just in circumstance, but in consciousness. Transitions shapes change and transformation.

Key Takeaway

  • Change is an event—transition is a process. Recognizing this distinction allows us to move through life with emotional and psychological clarity.
  • Transformation is deeply rooted change. It’s not merely about adapting, it’s about reshaping our beliefs, values, and identity.
  • Transitions shapes change and transformation. They provide spaces where resilience is cultivated, clarity emerges, and profound change becomes possible.

Thoughts and Reflection Corner

  • Can you recall a time you experienced a change, transition, or transformation?
  • What systems or habits in your life are ripe for transformation—not just a change?
